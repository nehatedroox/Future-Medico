OVER VIEW

Pharmacy is a great career option that has a strong scope with many lucrative opportunities. The projection is that the pharmacy industry will reach $55 billion at a CAGR of 22.4% by 2020. With such strong numbers, B Pharmacy admission makes up for a lucrative career for students. B.Pharma is a four-year-long undergraduate course that deals with the testing and researching drugs for a particular disease and giving awareness about the dosage of drugs to the patients. The B.Pharmacy course presents the students with a lot of job opportunities. One of the main tasks of a pharmacist is to prescribe medications to patients. It is evident from this very fact that the course of B Pharm focuses on the study of biology, pharmaceutical chemistry (inorganic, physical and organic), biochemistry, medicines, physiology, pharmacology, dispensing, etc. The regulatory body for the program is the Pharmacy Council of India (PCI), which is responsible for laying down all norms and regulations for the B.Pharma course. Proper understanding of medical drugs and their distribution is essential, so the pharmacist’s job scope is in greater demand.

REQUISITES

1. Required Education.

Minimum percentage: 50% in 10+2 from a recognized board. Should have studied Physics, Chemistry, and Biology in 10+2.

2. Projected Job Growth.

After the completion of this degree, the students can practice as pharmacists. Pharmacists can work in a range of industries related to the prescription, manufacture & provision of medicines.

3. Median Salary

Salary Scale for B. Pharm Course: The average salary for a pharmacist is INR 2 to 5 Lakhs per year. It may range between INR 2,00,000 - INR 5,00,000 per annum.

Program Guide

  • Program Details

    • Bachelor of Pharmacy (B.Pharma) is a four-year-long course and is mainly divided into 6-8 semesters, depending upon the coursework and practical sessions. B.Pharma imparts useful knowledge about biochemical science and the healthcare business. Students during their coursework have to perform various practicals and experiments. students study the methods and processes of preparing drugs and how the dispensing of medicine and drugs occurs. For anyone who aspires to be a pharmacist today, they must undertake this course so that they have a thorough knowledge of the science of medicine. The course enables candidates to practice the profession of a Pharmacist after graduation. B.Pharmacy degree has a lot of scope and is going to flourish more in the coming years as there is a great demand for healthcare professionals who can prepare drugs to battle several diseases and deficiencies.

  • Online Degree

    • Pharm can't be pursued from distance education/learning mode as the D. Pharm/B. Pharm degree awarded by University or Autonomous institution stands to be invalid. As per the PCI norms, all Pharmacy degrees ranging from Diploma to Ph.D. should be pursued through regular mode only.

Educational Requirements and Admissions

Students need to complete 10+2 with a minimum aggregate of 50% from the recognized boards. B.Pharma’s age limit is around 25 years old and the minimum age to apply for the course is 17 years. B.Pharm admission is given to the aspiring students based on their merit via an entrance exam conducted through different universities and colleges.

Salary Statistics and Employment outlook

Career Scope & Job Opportunities in the Pharmacy field are huge. The sales & Marketing job sector also hires a maximum number of pharmacy graduates. One can also work in retail stores of medicines. After registering with the state Pharmacy Council, you can open a chemist/drug shop and sell medicines as per doctor’s prescription. According to Payscale, the average starting salary after B.Pharm is INR 2- 5 LPA. This B. Pharmacy salary in India is not fixed and changes due to many factors. If students obtain some work experience or higher education, it can help them get a higher salary at work. It is a very good course and is job-oriented. It provides diverse job opportunities in both Government and Private sectors. The employment of pharmacists is projected to decline 3 percent from 2019 to 2029. Many pharmacists work in retail pharmacies and drug stores, which are expected to lose jobs as more people fill their prescriptions via mail order and online.
