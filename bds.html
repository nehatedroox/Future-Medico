OVER VIEW

BDS is one of the most popular and designated degrees of dentists (doctors). The BDS (Bachelor of Dental Science) is the only educational and professional program for dental surgery in India. It is equivalent to the MBBS and owes the “Dr” domain. In the medical educational field, it is the second choice of the students after the MBBS course. It enables an aspirant to pursue a career as a dentist. The field of Medicine is always evergreen and full of opportunities. The scope of employment and a large number of jobs for a Bachelor of Dental Science in this sector is rising. It also grants the aspirants an opportunity to serve society and opens up a broad scope of employment for a noble profession.
Dental science is focused on the rehabilitation of the teeth and their other functions. Oral Medicine and Radiology are the new areas of dental science that are increasingly being targeted as the limelight while offering lucrative job opportunities to aspirants of the course.

REQUISITES

1. Required Education.

The students, who have passed the 10+2 examination with science stream and have Physics, Chemistry, and Biology and English in 12th stream, may go for the BDS course.

2. Projected Job Growth.

Dental care is very much important and so are BDS doctors or dentists as they are called, who help the patients to maintain good hygiene. BDS graduates can find jobs very easily as they have the opportunity to start their clinics or to work in hospitals.

3. Median Salary

The average BDS salary is INR 3.05 Lakh per annum. This salary varies based on the sector of employment of the aspirant as well as his or her diligence, dedication, and seniority.

Program Guide

  • Program Details

    • BDS or Bachelor of Dental Science course is a 4 years bachelor’s dentistry course. BDS Course duration can be extended up to 5 years if the student wishes to opt for an internship. BDS focuses on dental health and hygiene. Bachelor of Dental Science is the only course where the BDS syllabus is framed by Indian health care is recommended in India. The syllabus of the BDS course comprises group discussions, seminars, clinical work, academic lectures, demonstrations, and conferences. Candidates are educated in the fields of dental treatment, including dental anatomy and oral histology, oral pathology, community dentistry, surgical procedures, dental materials, Pedodontics, oral medicine & radiology, etc.

  • Online Degree

    • It is possible to become a dentist by taking online courses, in choice areas or degrees. After this, one must obtain certification from the necessary regulating bodies.

Educational Requirements and Admissions

The eligibility criteria to gain admission into a dental school include A pass certificate in 10+2 with English, Physics, Chemistry, and Biology as compulsory subjects A minimum of 50% marks is required. Admission to BDS is granted through a national competitive examination called NEET. Students who are interested in BDS courses can get admissions by writing entrance exams of different states or universities. Students can also write to get admissions to BDS colleges.

Salary Statistics and Employment outlook

BDS course grants the aspirants an opportunity to serve society and opens up a broad scope of employment for a noble profession. This valuation varies based on the sector of employment of the aspirant as well as his or her diligence, dedication, and seniority/ experience at the workplace. FAQ

MBBS: Full form: Bachelor of Medicine and Bachelor of Surgery Focal point: The MBBS course focuses on all the anatomical aspects of the body except the dental issue including gynecology, pediatrics, psychiatry, orthopedics, cardiology, urology, oncology, endocrinology, plastic surgery, ophthalmology, anaesthesiology. BDS: Full form: Bachelor of Dental Science Focal point: The BDS course focuses on the dental aspect of human anatomy as well as dental treatment including surgical procedures, dental materials, dental anatomy, and oral histology, oral pathology, community dentistry, Pedodontics, oral medicine & radiology.

Yes, BDS is a very prospective career option with a large number of potential job opportunities in a large number of hospitals, dental clinics, and nursing homes

MBBS and BDS are equally difficult and equally easy. MBBS stands for Bachelor of Medicine, Bachelor of Surgery. ... Whereas after BDS you become a dentist and a super-specialist after MDS. If you don't want to go for higher studies after MBBS then you should choose BDS.

BDS stands for Bachelor of Dental Surgery. It is a 5 years long undergraduate course. 12th Science stream Biology group students are eligible to pursue this professional course after completing their 12th standard.

Yes, there is a bridge course from BDS to MBBS, Public Universities are starting with the course. Public Universities will also be allowed to provide the option of a Bridge Course. After completion of BDS, this bridge is expected to be three years in duration.

A BDS graduate can only pursue a master's degree in dentistry. If a BDS graduate wants to build a career in surgery, he/ she can opt for MDS in oral and maxillofacial surgery.
