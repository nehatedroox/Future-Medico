OVERVIEW

Established in 1979, the Ramaiah Medical College (RMC) is affiliated to the Rajiv Gandhi University of Health Sciences (RGUHS), Karnataka, and recognized by the Medical Council of India (MCI). the vision of providing quality education to all.Equipped with the latest infrastructure, technology-enabled classrooms, and advanced laboratories, the College primarilyfocuses on teaching, clinical practice, and research to train the next generation of doctors.

How is Education at Ramaiah Medical College?

The teachers and the management of the Ramaiah Medical College aim at providing value-basededucation to the students. The students are trained to become individuals who can transform society by becoming making others socially conscious and responsible. The curriculum is crafted to teach students about health, development, human values, compassion, sympathy, sincerity, and honesty by focusing on learning by doing. Under the Interdisciplinary Community Orientation Programme, the students interact with village panchayat members and local residents together with students of other medical sciences (Physiotherapy, Nursing, Dental, Pharmacy) and visiting the community health centers and interacting with mentally disabled children. MISSION & VISION

MISSIONThe institution will provide high-quality medical education in compliance with regulatory criteria, while simultaneously considering societal needs and worldwide significance. The Institution will cultivate an environment that encourages research.

VISSIONTo be a national and international centre for higher-quality medical education, service, and research.

PLACEMENT

Placement Since this is a medical college, there is no provision for placing the students in the formal sense, and theCollege does not have placement cell-like colleges of other disciplines. The students after completion of their MBBS degree can practice as general practitioners provided they are registered under a State government. The students can work as junior doctors in medical colleges, and after passing out, they can work as medical consultants, doctors with various hospitals, and private clinics. They can also work as private practitioners as well.

FACULTY

The College has very experienced and highly qualified faculties to impart quality education to the students. All the faculties are Post Graduates in their respective professions, and more than half of them hold Doctorate degrees.

EXAMS

All admissions are conducted on the basis of marks obtained in NEET conducted by the Central Board of Secondary Education.For admission to PG courses, students need to appear for the All IndiaPG Entrance Test and obtain the relevant cutoff marks.

ADMISSION

The College offers Graduation and Post – Graduation courses such as MBBS, MD, and Diploma courses in Anatomy, Physiology, Microbiology, Pediatrics, Gastronomy and many other branches. The admission is given on the basis of NEET and other All India Examinations for various courses like BPT, BDS, Nursing and other courses. Some of the courses also require the candidate to appear for personal interview rounds. The entrance examinations are usually held around the month of May and June.

CAMPUS

The students get to enjoy the best campus life among any college in Bangalore. The hostel committee of each campus has made sure of all needed facilities for the best academic outcome. The dynamic campus life pushes the students towards healthier, focused, and with all modern education amenities. At the same time to ensure safety and discipline for all the students, the college has the necessary restrictions and rules in place which are well enforced by the hostel authorities. The college also has well-maintained and affordable hostel facilities for students. In addition to that, there is a chapel situated on the college premises for the students to worship and pray. The college has criteria and a well-equipped library which remains open every day for the convenience of the students. There are also ample opportunities for sports with the college having two basketball and one volleyball court. The seventy-acre campus has a food street that serves food from various food joints within a considerable budget.

FACILITIES

HOSTEL-

The college has separate hostels for boys and girls. The boy’s hostel has a capacity of 310 while the girls’ hostel has a capacity of 640. Both the hostels have well furnished, high-class amenities to give the feel of home away from home

STUDENT EXCHANGE PROGRAM-

To help the student's desire to cater to global communities after they have become professionals, the College provides opportunities to undergraduate students to visit foreign universities with which they have a Memorandum of Understanding signed. This helps the students to understand and experience global standards of medical practice and understand different cultures and practices. As a part of the Student Exchange Program, foreign students also visit the College and foreign professors give periodic visits to the College as guest lecturers

CENTRAL LIBRARY–

The college has a central library that is spread across 46,000 square feet which is well maintained and regularly enriches its collection to allow users full access to current research tools and technologies and at the same time being accessible to students, residents, and staff. The library is a member of “HELINET” consortium which offers more than 675 indexed national and international journals. The library has sixty-two computers with internet access and facilities for scanning, printing, and photocopying. The library has more than fifteen thousand books in back volumes to meet the needs of the students.

INFRASTRUCTURE

ADVANCED LEARNING CENTER:

The Advanced Learning Center (ALC) has been established to provide hands-on training and organize workshops where students are taught basic and advanced surgical skills as well as teach specific methods of surgeries within orthopedics, ENT, invasive procedures, neurosurgery, general surgery It is always accessible by students, faculty and the nursing staff. It also organizes frequent workshops and implant validation programs.

OPERATION LABS:

There are two types of labs in the College.

  1. Medical Skills Lab- Here the students are taught the practical basics of surgery, life support, advanced cardiac life support, and other skills at post-graduate, graduate, and nursing levels using mannequins, models, and simulation techniques.
  2. Cadaveric Research and Training Unit– Here the students get to opportunity to have specific training to hone their practical skills

FAQ

What is the fee for MBBS in India?

The MBBS course fee may vary from government, private and deemed universities. The average MBBS fee for government medical colleges in India ranges from INR 20,000 to INR 7.5 lakh, whereas the MBBS fee for private colleges can range from INR 20 lakh to more than INR 1 Cr.

What happens when we fail in NEET?

If you fail NEET, then you will not get admission into good government medical colleges. Also, as per the new rule implemented by the CBSE, you can only give this exam thrice. So, make sure you don't lose your three precious chances.

Can I join MBBS without NEET?

Though it is not possible to get admission in MBBS without NEET in India. NEET UG exam is mandatory for admission in MBBS in India or abroad. But there are various other medical courses which you can join on the basis of class 12th marks.

Why is MBBS so Costly?

MBBS education is expensive in India mainly due to the factor that there is more demand than supply of the number of medical seats.

How is life after MBBS?

Well life after MBBS is neither very easy and nor very hard also.There are many points.

How can I do MBBS after the 12th?

The students, who have cleared the 10+2 examination and they had the Physics, Chemistry and Biology in 12th stream, may go for the MBBS degree course. The MBBS is the only course in India to become a medical doctor.
