OVER VIEW

Nursing is a healthcare profession that is focused on the care of individuals, families, and communities to help them attend a healthy living. “Nursing is the medical profession for administration and taking care of the patient with the help of healthcare science”. The students of BSC nursing will be trained to work in different specialties.
The course is designed and organized to train and qualify the students in different fields. Specialists have established a curriculum for B.Sc Nursing, which the government and private body follow. There are many objectives of B.Sc in Nursing, including critical thinking; nurses will be trained in this course with a clear vision and abilities for being caring, assertive, responding to various needs of the industry, health care department, and society. Nurses may be differentiated from other health care providers by their approach to patient care, training, and scope of practice. Nurses practice in many specialties with differing levels of prescription authority.

REQUISITES

1. Required Education.

Pass in 10+2, or equivalent with Physics, Chemistry, Biology, and English and a minimum of 45% marks taken together in Physics, Chemistry, Biology, and English.

2. Projected Job Growth.

B.Sc Nursing is a course that enables aspirants to understand how to be attentive and think and make quick decisions at the time of critical situations. After graduating from this course students have the scope to pursue masters as well in the same field. The students holding this degree are highly in demand by both private and government sectors.

3. Median Salary

Graduates of B.Sc Nursing graduates can earn up to INR 3.2 Lakhs per Annum to INR 7.8 Lakhs per Annum. The amount may differ from employer, organization, and hospital.

Program Guide

  • Program Details

    • Bachelor of Science in nursing is a four-year-long undergraduate course that deals with nursing and medicine as primary functions and the course aims to enable students to become future nurses and contribute positively to the medical field. Undergraduate Programme Nursing is of paramount importance for the nurses, as it enables them to become exemplary citizens by adhering to the code of ethics and professional conduct at all times in fulfilling personal, social, and professional obligations and responding to national aspirations. This Undergraduate Programme is based on the values of caring and aims to help individuals to attain independence in self-care. It helps the candidates develop compassion and an understanding of human behavior. It encourages the practitioners to provide care with respect, dignity and protect the rights of individuals and groups. It recognizes national health goals and is committed to participating in the implementation of National Health policies and programs.

  • Online Degree

    • An online BSN program can be completed in as little as 15 months. Most courses are online to allow students to work at their own pace and continue their employment. Some schools require clinical experience in a healthcare facility, but students may be able to complete these requirements in their hometowns

FAQ

Nursing graduates will have to follow the same procedure to apply for MBBS as 12th pass candidates. You must appear for the NEET entrance exam and clear it to get admission. Admission in the second year or lateral entry is not possible for the MBBS course. No college in India offers lateral entry for MBBS.

Nursing is one of the noblest professions and one of the best fields if you want to serve the community. Medicine, being one of the most recession-proof industries, offers very good career prospects and job opportunities for doctors and nurses alike.

Neet is not compulsory for the BSC Nursing courses. There is a separate entrance Examination for BSC nursing in different universities and colleges. You need to appear for the entrance exam. Another eligibility criteria are to have passed xii Science with a minimum of 50% marks in PCB subjects with English as a compulsory subject.

There's lots of learning, the exams are challenging, schedules are tricky, assignments constantly pile one on top of the over. All these have the potential of making your student life hard. Of course, for every nursing student, the experience and the training process go differently.

If a draft proposal of the Indian Nursing Council gets approved, students with arts and commerce backgrounds will also be able to pursue a BSc Nursing course.
